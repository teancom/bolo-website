License

Bolo is licensed under the GNU General Public License (v3). The goal is to ensure that the software remains beneficial to the IT community and people interested in building monitoring systems. On top of that, Bolo makes use of other GPL'd software, notably 0MQ.

Contributions

As Open Source software, Bolo grows and improves by way of contributions from multiple developers, some working for different companies, in different industries and on different continents. In order to respect the copyright of individual contributors, and to ensure that we have the legal right to distribute the whole software under the terms of the GPL, we require that all contributors sign a Contributor License Agreement, or CLA.

The CLA grants us the right to distribute contributions to Bolo, lets you maintain your copyrights and patent claims, and protects the end users of Bolo as much as possible. Basically, it's a legal implement (not unlike the GPL itself) that ensures we can keep making Bolo available as F/OSS.

Everyone contributing legally significant (i.e. not typo corrections, whitespace changes, etc.) must have a signed CLA on file. Those will be kept on this page, in the Signed CLAs section, for public record.

The CLA comes in two flavors: Individual and Corporate.

Most people will want the Individual Contributor License Agreement (often abbreviated to just ICLA). The Software Grant And Coroprate Contributor License Agreement, or CCLA, lets corporate entities enter into an agreement that covers work done by named employees.

Signing Process

It's simple.

  1. Download a copy of either the ICLA or the CCLA
  2. Print it out
  3. Fill it out (in ink)
  4. Scan / photograph it
  5. Email the PDF per the instructions in the agreement
  6. Go write some code

You are of course free to start working on a pull request before signing the CLA, but you will need to get us the paperwork before we can merge your changes, no matter who splendiferous the code is.
